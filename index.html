About

I am a technical content writer who specializes in making bleeding-edge technology accessible without dumbing it down. For over two years, I have been writing long-form B2B content on topics most writers avoid: Edge AI model optimization, physical AI for humanoid robots, embedded computer vision, and wearable health devices.

I am part of the technical writing team at embedUR, an AI SaaS company in the embedded systems space, where I own my content pillar within the pipeline from research to publication. Before AI, I wrote technical content in the Web3 and cryptocurrency space.

My background in English Literature gives me something most technical writers lack: a philosophy of craft. I believe every text needs soul. Not personal opinion, but human presence in the structure, the pacing, and the choices. The audience is always the star of the show.

How I Work

My approach to technical content

Every piece follows the same discipline, whether it is 500 words or 5,000.

01

Research First, Always

I read everything before writing anything. Primary sources, academic papers, engineering docs, product specs. The depth of the research determines the authority of the content.

02

Audience as the Star

Technical detail is the backdrop, not the headline. Every concept gets grounded in a familiar analogy. Every paragraph earns its place. If the reader has to reread a sentence, the writer failed.

03

Clarity Over Cleverness

No one cares about literary quality if the copy is not clear enough to understand first. Hook with the metaphor, then deliver the precision. Every word is a deliberate choice.
